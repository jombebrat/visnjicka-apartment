Stay Connected

SIM Cards at the Airport

  • 📱
    Buy at the airport: SIM cards are available immediately after baggage claim at the arrivals hall. Look for Telenor and A1 kiosks — both are reliable networks with good coverage across Serbia.
  • 💰
    Cost: A prepaid SIM with ~10 GB of data costs around 500–700 RSD (~5–6€). You can top up easily at any kiosk (trafika) in the city.
  • 🛂
    What you need: Your passport. Registration is quick — usually done in a few minutes at the counter.
  • 🌍
    EU roaming note: If you have an EU SIM, roaming in Serbia may be expensive — Serbia is not in the EU. A local SIM is strongly recommended for longer stays.
  • 📶
    Coverage: Both Telenor and A1 have excellent 4G/LTE coverage in Belgrade and across the country. 5G is available in central Belgrade.
  • 🔁
    Alternatively: If the airport kiosks are closed (late night arrivals), you can also buy SIMs the next morning at any phone shop or large supermarket in the city.
  • 🏠
    Don't forget: The apartment WiFi is 300 Mbps — fast enough for video calls and streaming. You may not even need mobile data much during your stay.
Getting Around

Local transport

🚌
Public Transport — Free!

Since January 2025, all buses, trams, trolleybuses and BG:Voz trains in Belgrade are completely free of charge for everyone. Just hop on. Bus stop "Vuka Vrčevića" is 2 min walk — line 16 goes directly to city center in ~15 min.

📱
Taxi Apps

CarGo is the main local taxi app — reliable, price shown upfront. Most rides within the city cost 300–600 RSD. Always preferred over flagging a taxi on the street.

🚲
Bike & Scooter

NextBike bike sharing has stations around the city. Electric scooters (Bolt, Lime) are also widely available. Great for exploring Kalemegdan fortress and the riverfront.

Right Next Door

Big Fashion Shopping Mall

One of Belgrade's biggest and most popular shopping malls is practically on your doorstep — Big Fashion is a 10-minute walk along Višnjička street, or one bus stop on lines 16, 23, or 25.

🛍️
Shopping

Over 100 stores including Zara, H&M, Reserved, Pull&Bear, Bershka, Mango, New Yorker, Sport Vision and many more. Open daily 10:00–22:00. Great for a rainy day.

🍽️
Food & Cafés

Large food court on the top floor with fast food and sit-down options. Kafeterija specialty coffee inside the mall is excellent. Also IDEA supermarket on the lower level — open 9:00–22:00.

🎬
Entertainment

Cinema, bookstores, game stores and a kids play area. Lidl supermarket is just next door (Višnjička 94), and Maxi supermarket is a 5 min walk behind the mall.

🍷

Ada Huja riverfront (5 min walk): Right behind Big Fashion, along the Danube, you'll find Caffe Restaurant X and Caffe Dilema — both rated 4.4–4.5★, great food, beautiful river views. Perfect for lunch, dinner, or evening drinks. A true local gem most tourists miss.

Food & Drink

Where to eat & drink

🥩
Serbian Classics

Don't miss ćevapi (grilled minced meat), pljeskavica (Serbian burger), and burek (flaky pastry with meat or cheese). Skadarlija street has the best traditional kafanas — try Tri Šešira or Dva Jelena.

☕
Coffee Culture

Belgrade has a vibrant café scene. Savamala district is the creative hub with specialty coffee shops. Locally, try the cafés along Ada Huja riverbank — great views, relaxed atmosphere, 5 min from the apartment.

🍺
Nightlife

Belgrade is famous for its nightlife. Savamala for bars and clubs, Skadarlija for a bohemian night out. River clubs ("splavovi") on the Sava are uniquely Belgrade. Most places open late and stay open very late.

🛒
Groceries Nearby

Maxi supermarket is the closest large store, about 10 min walk. Idea and Lidl are also nearby. For fresh produce, the Karaburma pijaca (market) is a great local experience.

🌅
Ada Huja (5 min away)

Your closest riverside escape. Outdoor cafés, a go-kart track, tennis courts, and a beach area in summer. Perfect for a morning walk or an afternoon coffee. Locals love this spot.

💡
Good to Know

Tipping is common — 10% is standard. Most places accept cards but smaller kafanas may be cash only. Lunch is typically 12–15h, dinner from 19h onwards. Restaurants are open late.

Sightseeing

What to see & do

  • 🏰
    Kalemegdan Fortress — Belgrade's most iconic landmark. Free entry, stunning views over the Danube and Sava rivers. Best at sunset. ~20 min by bus.
  • 🎭
    Skadarlija — The bohemian quarter. Cobblestone street lined with kafanas, live music, and street artists. Best experienced in the evening. ~15 min by bus.
  • 🚶
    Knez Mihailova Street — The main pedestrian zone. Shopping, cafés, street performers. Free open-air museum of 19th century architecture. ~15 min by bus.
  • 🎨
    Savamala District — The creative hub. Street art, galleries, design studios, and the best bars in the city. Comes alive on weekends.
  • ⛪
    Saint Sava Temple — One of the largest Orthodox churches in the world. Impressive interior, free entry. Worth visiting even if not religious.
  • 🏊
    Ada Ciganlija — Belgrade's river island beach. Swimming, cycling, sports in summer. Very popular with locals. ~25 min by bus.
  • 🏛️
    National Museum — Reopened after renovation, excellent collection of Serbian and European art. Republic Square. ~15 min by bus.
  • 🌿
    Topčider Park — Beautiful 19th century park, perfect for a relaxed afternoon. Horse stables, cafés, peaceful walks.
  • 🌉
    Brankov Bridge walk — Walk across for great views of both rivers meeting. Best at golden hour.
  • 🎪
    Zemun — Historic town now part of Belgrade. Austro-Hungarian architecture, riverside promenade, excellent fish restaurants.
Practical Info

Good to know

💳
Money & Payments

Currency is Serbian Dinar (RSD). 1€ ≈ 117 RSD, $1 ≈ 108 RSD. Exchange at banks or official exchange offices — avoid hotel exchanges. ATMs are widely available. Cards accepted almost everywhere except small local markets.

📶
Internet & SIM

The apartment WiFi is 300 Mbps — more than enough for video calls and streaming. If you need mobile data, buy a prepaid SIM from Telenor or A1 at the airport or any phone shop. 10GB for ~500 RSD.

🏥
Health & Safety

Belgrade is generally very safe. Normal city precautions apply. Nearest pharmacy: within 5 min walk. For emergencies: KBC Zemun hospital is the closest major hospital (~15 min). Emergency number: 194.

🗣️
Language

Serbian is the official language. English is widely spoken in restaurants, shops, and by younger generations. Google Translate works well for menus. A few words locals appreciate: Hvala (Thank you), Molim (Please/You're welcome).

🌤️
Weather

Spring (Apr–May): 15–22°C, mild and lovely. Summer (Jun–Aug): 25–38°C, hot. Autumn (Sep–Oct): 15–25°C, golden and pleasant. Winter (Nov–Mar): 0–8°C, can snow. The apartment has AC and heating for all seasons.

🕐
Belgrade Timing

Belgrade runs on its own schedule — lunch is 13–15h, dinner rarely before 20h. Nightlife doesn't start until midnight. Shops open late (~10h) and many are open on Sundays. Banks close at 17h on weekdays.
